What is Prime Factorization?

Prime factorization is the process of breaking a number into a product of prime numbers. Prime numbers are numbers greater than 1 that have exactly two factors: 1 and itself.

Prime Factorization Method

  • Divide the number by the smallest prime (2, 3, 5, ...)
  • Continue dividing until quotient becomes 1
  • Write the product of all prime divisors

Why Prime Factorization is Important?

  • Foundation of number theory
  • Used in HCF and LCM calculations
  • Essential for school & competitive exams
  • Helps simplify fractions and algebraic expressions

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can 1 be a prime factor?
No, 1 is not a prime number.

What if the number is prime?
The number itself is the only prime factor.
